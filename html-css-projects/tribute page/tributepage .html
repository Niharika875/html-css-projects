A Tribute to A.P.J Abdul Kalam

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam) was a renowned Indian scientist, aerospace engineer, and the 11th President of India (2002–2007). He is widely respected as the "Missile Man of India" for his significant contributions to the country's defense and space programs.

  • Born: October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu,India
  • Died: July 27, 2015, in Shillong, Meghalaya, India (while delivering a lecture)

Major Contributions

  1. Scientific Career:
    • Worked at DRDO (Defence Research and Development Organisation) and ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation).
    • Played a pivotal role in India’s missile development programs (Agni, Prithvi).
    • Was a key figure in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.
  2. President of India:
    • Served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.
    • Known as the “People’s President” due to his humble nature and ability to connect with people, especially youth.
  3. Author and Visionary:
    • Wrote many inspiring books including:
      • Wings of Fire (his autobiography)
      • Ignited Minds
      • India 2020
